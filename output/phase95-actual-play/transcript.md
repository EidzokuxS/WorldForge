## Opening

The rope bridge to the central exchange hall creaks underfoot, fibers humming with the current that tugs at the reef platforms below. Seven halls fan out across the shallow water—coral walls bleached white by salt and moonlight, their pearl-inlaid counters visible through open archways where licensed notaries bend over transaction chains. The smell hits next: brine and cuttlefish ink, the chemical bite of receipt wax heating over small copper pots.

A broker at the nearest counter stamps a memory receipt with a hollow thock that echoes across the water. The notary beside him reaches for a fresh validation seal, fingers moving with the automatic precision of someone who has certified a thousand transaction chains before the moon-pull shifts.

The reef platforms hold steady. For now. The gaps between them are narrow—three feet, maybe four—but the water in those channels runs fast and dark, pulled toward theMoon-Churned Reaches where the current deepens. A notice board near the second hall lists verification rates in chalked numerals that someone has recently altered: the old prices crossed out, new ones written above.

The bridge sways. Not much. Enough to feel the tension in the ropes, the weight of the crossing, the fact that the moon-pull has not yet peaked.
