# NPCs, Factions, And Consequences

WorldForge's living-world systems are about making the campaign feel like it has people and pressures beyond the player's current sentence.

## NPC Tiers

Not every NPC gets the same treatment.

## Key NPCs

Key NPCs are important enough to have stronger continuity:

- Goals.
- Memories.
- Private knowledge.
- Relationships.
- Plans or ongoing pressure.
- Reasons to act when the player is near or when a world thread reaches them.

They are closer to co-players than scenery.

## Persistent NPCs

Persistent NPCs remain real in the world but do not need a full independent decision every turn. A shopkeeper, guard captain, witness, or local contact may live here unless the story makes them more central.

## Temporary NPCs

Temporary NPCs can appear to make a scene work:

- A gondolier.
- A clerk.
- A nervous witness.
- A street vendor.
- A guard at a gate.

If play keeps focusing on them, they can become more important.

## Factions

Factions are organized forces. They help the world move when the player is not personally pushing every event.

A faction can have:

- Goals.
- Resources.
- Territory.
- Operations.
- Reports.
- Internal command chains.
- Rivalries.
- Reasons to react to public events.

Examples:

- A ministry starts covering up ghost testimony failures.
- A clan sends scouts after a strange power appears.
- A criminal network spreads a false rumor to flush out a witness.

## World Threads

A world thread is an ongoing situation that can progress over time.

Examples:

- A missing-person investigation.
- A faction raid being prepared.
- A disease spreading through a district.
- A ritual approaching completion.
- A political scandal moving toward exposure.

Threads let the world have continuity beyond the immediate room.

## Consequences

Consequences can be direct or delayed.

Direct:

- You break a door.
- Someone sees you.
- You move to a new location.
- You gain an injury.

Delayed:

- A rumor spreads.
- A faction decides you are useful or dangerous.
- A witness changes their story.
- An NPC leaves before you return.
- A clue becomes harder to access.

Good play often comes from delayed consequences returning at the worst possible moment.

## What Happens Offscreen

WorldForge does not try to secretly rewrite everything while you are away. Instead, it tracks the important offscreen pressures and brings them forward when they become relevant.

This keeps the world alive without turning every turn into a slow simulation of the entire setting.
