# FAQ

## Is WorldForge A Game Or A Writing Tool?

It is closer to a text RPG sandbox. You play a character, make choices, and the world reacts. It can be used creatively, but the main fantasy is play, not just co-writing prose.

## Do I Need To Know Programming?

No, not to use the app once it is running. You do need basic comfort with installing Node.js, running `npm install`, and starting the local app.

## Does The World Really Continue Without Me?

The goal is not to simulate every person constantly. The game focuses on important NPCs, factions, active world threads, and consequences that can matter to play.

## Can I Use Existing Fictional Worlds?

Yes. Be specific about the source, timeline, and what changed. Crossovers work best when you explain which rules or power systems matter.

## Can I Play An Overpowered Character?

Yes, but power should have context. A very strong character can still face political pressure, hidden information, social consequences, preparation problems, stronger opponents, or collateral risk.

## Can I Lie To NPCs?

Yes. A lie should be treated as a claim your character makes, not as automatic truth.

## Can The Game Make Mistakes?

Yes. This is an active project, and AI-driven play can be messy. Use review, retry, checkpoints, and clearer actions to steer it.

## Where Are Campaigns Stored?

Campaigns are stored locally on your machine. The repository uses a `campaigns/` folder for local campaign data.

## Do I Need Image Generation?

No. Images are optional. The core game is text-first.

## What Makes WorldForge Different From A Normal Chatbot?

WorldForge tries to separate what the narrator writes from what the world records as true. The game should track state, consequences, claims, secrets, memories, and visible information instead of letting every cool sentence rewrite reality.
