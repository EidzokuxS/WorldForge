# Creating A Campaign

A campaign starts with an idea. WorldForge expands that idea into a playable world with places, factions, NPCs, lore, and a starting scene.

## The Campaign Idea

The best premise answers at least one of these questions:

- What kind of world is this?
- What is already tense or unstable?
- What makes this setting different from a generic fantasy/sci-fi world?
- What kind of stories should be possible here?

You do not need to write a full setting bible. A strong paragraph is enough.

## World DNA

World DNA is the short list of ideas that make the campaign feel like itself.

Examples:

- Magic leaves legal evidence.
- Memories can be traded, but only once.
- Factions know the prophecy is fake, but still need people to believe it.
- The strongest characters are not always politically safe to use.

DNA helps the generator stay focused. It is also useful later when you review whether a location, faction, or NPC belongs in the world.

## Known Worlds And Crossovers

WorldForge can work with familiar settings, alternate timelines, and crossovers. For these, be clear about:

- The franchise or source world.
- The time period.
- What has changed.
- Which power systems or rules matter.
- What should remain recognizable.

Example:

> Naruto after the Chunin Exams, but cursed energy from Jujutsu Kaisen has begun appearing in border villages.

This tells the game the source, timeline, change, and likely pressure point.

## WorldBooks

WorldBooks are optional reference material. They can help when you want the game to use a specific lore base, homebrew setting, or imported notes.

Use them when:

- The setting has a lot of names or rules.
- You already have campaign notes.
- You want more consistency than a premise alone can provide.

You can still play without a WorldBook.

## Reviewing The World

After generation, read the world review before you start:

- Does the premise still match your idea?
- Are the main locations interesting?
- Do factions have understandable goals?
- Do important NPCs have motives, not just names?
- Is there a good starting location?
- Is anything obviously wrong, duplicated, or too vague?

Small edits here can make the first play session much better.

## When To Regenerate

Regenerate a section when it misses the point. Edit manually when the idea is good but needs polish.

Regenerate:

- A faction has no usable goal.
- Locations feel unrelated to the premise.
- The world ignores a core DNA idea.

Edit:

- A name is awkward.
- A description needs one clearer sentence.
- A faction is good but needs a sharper motive.
