Geography
A generation ship named Maria Augusta has been in transit for 612 years. Its destination is still treated as sacred doctrine, but the surviving records no longer agree on where the ship is actually going.
Political Structure
A cloistered order governs the waking decks and keeps 60,000 sleepers under ritual authority. Their power is administrative, religious, and brutally practical.
Central Conflict
The central argument is whether mercy means preserving the original sleeping order or breaking that order before the cargo becomes unrecoverable.
Cultural Flavor
The world speaks in shipboard liturgy, rationed tenderness, bureaucratic guilt, and short acts of defiance performed where cameras cannot see.
Environment
The playable geography is a vertical vessel: twelve decks, a chapel-spine, sealed archives, cargo wheels, garden machinery, and maintenance corridors that function like neighborhoods.
Wildcard
The impossible pressure is that the ship has begun to dream, and some sleepers repeat names they were never taught.

i. of ix

On the Law of Mercy

The first Custodes wrote: “The mercy is to keep them sleeping until landfall.” Mother Selene’s order has obeyed for 612 years. The unredacted charter ends differently — “…or until the ship can no longer reach landfall, in which case the mercy is to wake them.” No one alive but Veran has read the second clause.

ii.

The Ship’s First Dream

Three months ago, in the dim, three Custodes simultaneously experienced sleep-paralysis lasting four minutes. Each saw the same image: a young woman walking the corridor at L10, mouth moving, no sound. None of them knew her. Tova knew her. It was Veran.

iii.

The Captain

Captain Theryn died in year nine, not the 600 the order teaches. He died of grief, two years after he understood the math: the chosen sun would not be there when they arrived. He left a sealed log. He left a daughter. He left this charter Veran has now read.

iv.

The Sun That Won’t Be There

The Maria was sent to a star whose lifetime calculation, redone in year 47 by an unnamed engineer, fell catastrophically short. The order has known this for three centuries and has not told anyone. Including itself.

v.

What the Born Dream Of

The 38 children of the order have begun to share a single dream: a field, an open horizon, a fig tree. They have never seen any of these things. The fig tree is the captain’s. The field is not on any star.

vi.

The First Recidivist

Year 411. A Custodes named Mira woke a sleeper named Iol because she could not bear his slow scream in the dim. She confessed, was tried, was forgiven. The case was struck from the record. Veran has read the unstruck version.

vii.

Why the Silent Stopped Speaking

In year 184, the Engine Heart began to make a sound that frightened the engineers tending it. They could not describe it without weeping. They took a vow of silence rather than be required to. The sound has not stopped. It has gotten louder.

viii.

The Listening Room

The place on deck 3 where the ship dreams loudest. Sealed since year 503. The seal is rated for fifty more years. The seal has begun to bleed.

ix.

What Iru Doesn’t Know

Iru has not been told that he was supposed to be the next captain. The lineage runs through him. He has not been told that the woman who woke him is the one who would have been his daughter, in another life, on another ship.

Geography
The Maria Augusta — a generation ship, year 612 of flight, en route to a star that no longer burns.
Political Structure
A cloistered religious order — the Custodes — sworn to keep 60,000 sleepers until landfall.
Central Conflict
Mercy as obedience versus mercy as transgression. The vow against the truth.
Cultural Flavor
Liturgical · quietly furious · sparse. Long sentences when grief earns them. Short sentences when violence demands them.
Environment

Wildcard
